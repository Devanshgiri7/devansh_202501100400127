KERELA BAKCWATERS

Journey Through Nature’s Paradise. The Kerala Backwaters are a serene network of canals, rivers, lagoons, and lakes that weave through the lush landscapes of “God’s Own Country.” Among the most popular backwater destinations are Alleppey (Alappuzha), often called the “Venice of the East”, and Kumarakom, known for its tranquil beauty and rich birdlife. Cruising on a traditional houseboat through these palm-fringed waterways offers a unique glimpse into Kerala’s rural charm—passing by emerald paddy fields, rustic villages, and vibrant coconut groves. The backwaters are not just a natural wonder but also a cultural experience, where life flows gently along the water’s edge. Whether it’s a romantic escape, a family holiday, or a wellness retreat, the Kerala Backwaters promise an unforgettable journey into peace and harmony.
